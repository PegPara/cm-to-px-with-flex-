What is DPI?

DPI stands for Dots Per Inch. It measures the resolution or density of printed dots or pixels on a display or printer.

The higher the DPI, the more dots per inch, resulting in better detail and sharper images.

The lower the DPI, the fewer dots, which means less detail but smaller file sizes or faster printing.

How to Choose DPI?

  • Screens: Usually 96 dpi, sometimes 72 dpi on older or Apple devices.
  • Text Printing: Around 150–300 dpi for clear, readable documents.
  • Photo Printing: Usually 300–600 dpi for sharp images.
  • Archival Scanning or Art: 1200 dpi or more for finest detail.
